Every program, one sponsor

Whatever brought you here,
you belong here.

Whatever your program, your sponsor knows your world. The language, the steps, the traditions. It's all built in.

Debtors Anonymous (DA)
Money stress can quietly take over your whole life. DA is for people whose relationship with debt has become something they can't manage on their own, and who are ready to change that.
Business Debtors Anonymous (BDA)
For business owners whose relationship with business debt (credit lines, payroll, loans) has become something they can't manage alone. The pressure is different when others depend on you. BDA understands that.
Underearners Anonymous (UA)
For people who keep earning less than they need or want, not because of lack of talent, but because of patterns that run deeper. UA is for anyone ready to understand why, and to change it.
Alcoholics Anonymous (AA)
If alcohol has taken more from you than you ever meant to give, you're in the right place. Over 2 million people, in 180 countries, all walking the same road.
Al-Anon / Alateen
When someone you love is struggling, you need support too. Not just them. Al-Anon is for family and friends. Alateen is the same thing, built for younger members.
Narcotics Anonymous (NA)
For anyone whose relationship with drugs has become something they can't manage alone. No judgment, no lectures. Just people who genuinely understand what you're going through.
Overeaters Anonymous (OA)
When food has stopped being about hunger and started being about something else. Pain, comfort, escape. OA is for anyone who recognises that feeling and wants a way through it.
Gamblers Anonymous (GA)
For anyone who knows that one more bet was always going to be the last one. GA is a community of people who understand the pull and have found a way out of it.
Adult Children of Alcoholics (ACA)
Growing up around addiction or dysfunction leaves marks that don't just go away. ACA is for adults still carrying what they grew up with and who are ready to put some of it down.
Sex & Love Addicts Anonymous (SLAA)
For people whose patterns around sex, love, or relationships have started to feel like something they can't control. A confidential, judgment-free space where those patterns can be looked at honestly.
Workaholics Anonymous (WA)
For people whose relationship with work has become something they can't stop, even when it's costing them their health, their relationships, or their peace. WA is for anyone who recognises that work has become a way to avoid everything else.
