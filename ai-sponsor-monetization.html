AI Sponsor — how we make it more profitable

Ideas for monetizing AI Sponsor, grounded in what funded competitors are actually doing. Built for the product call — we'll keep adding to this.

Owner: Mariam · For: Matt · Started July 1, 2026 · Updated July 2 (current-situation projection + new research) · This is a working document, not a plan of record — flag anything you want cut or pushed.

The bet

The $5 subscription isn't the business — it's the front door. The money the market is finding is in what the AI routes people to: personalization they'll pay more for, human care at the right moment, keepsakes, and institutions who'll pay per-seat.

⚠️ Hard constraint: this is a sensitive audience and 12-step culture is wary of profiting off recovery. Every idea here is filtered to add genuine value to the person's recovery — never extract from vulnerability. That's not a nicety; it's what protects the trust the whole product runs on.

Where we stand today · July 2026

The honest picture with the beta cohort and the current deal

What the current setup costs us

The live backend runs on Claude Opus — the premium model tier ($5 in / $25 out per million tokens). At typical chat sizes that's roughly 2¢ per message. Per user per month: a casual user (~5 msgs/day) costs ~$3, a regular user (~15/day) ~$9, a heavy user (~30/day) ~$20+ — before caching.

With the 59-person beta cohort (growing toward ~150 as Matt keeps inviting), a normal engagement mix works out to roughly $250–500/month in AI cost, $0 revenue for the ~6-month free window. Total beta-phase burn ≈ $2–4K. That's the whole bill — fine to eat, exactly the "burn now" Matt is comfortable with.

The real risk isn't the bill — it's an outage. The Anthropic account has a $500/month spend cap. If beta engagement runs hot (and this is the one product where PMF signal is strong), we hit the cap mid-month and chat goes dark for every user — a repeat of the June 19 outage, but with real users in recovery on the other end. Fix: raise the cap + set alerts at 50/75/90%, and verify prompt caching is actually firing (it cuts input cost up to ~90%; console showed it not enabled in June).

What the current deal earns — and where it caps out

Right now: nothing is monetized. Stripe is in test mode, beta codes grant free access, and there's no live paid path. That's fine for now — but it also means this is the cheapest moment we'll ever have to change the pricing: no existing subscribers to grandfather, no "they raised prices on people in recovery" story. Whatever structure we want long-term should be the one we launch with.

The uncomfortable math: at $5 flat on the Opus model, the average user leaves ~$1–2/month of margin and a heavy user loses us ~$15/month. The current deal roughly breaks even at any scale — 100 users or 100,000. It's a front door, not a business, which is exactly the doc's thesis — but it means the levers below aren't optional extras; they're the actual revenue model.

The pricing pyramid solves itself with model tiering: run Standard ($5–9) on a fast, cheaper model (~0.4–1.3¢/msg → margin-positive even for heavy users) and reserve Opus + voice + custom voice + long-term memory for the $15–19 Premium tier. "Smarter model" as a premium feature is standard in the AI-companion market — that's Replika's exact structure.

The core insight

The AI is the front door to higher-value help

What the funded players actually do

Sunflower (YC / a16z) has openly moved past its subscription — its real revenue is a teletherapy clinic inside the app. The AI's data (relapse signals, mood, milestones, engagement drop-off) tells them exactly when a user needs a human, and they route that person to high-margin care. Reframe does a lighter version with its "Thrive Coaching" upsell. Dario Health grew its market by pivoting from "app" to "app + human services."

AI Sponsor sits with people every day and knows when they're struggling. That's a warm, perfectly-timed handoff — the most profitable and most on-mission lever we have, because it gets people real care.

Lever 1 · Pricing & packaging

Fixes the token-cost problem immediately

Add a $15–19 "Plus" tier

Free (capped messages) → $5–9 Standard → Premium with voice + a custom ElevenLabs voice, persistent memory/journal, unlimited, and the smartest model. Heavy users self-select into the tier that covers their token cost — which directly answers the "power user is upside-down at $5" worry.

Evidence: Replika Pro $19.99/mo (voice + memory + relationship modes). Loosid premium $12.99, I Am Sober $9.99, OpenRecovery $10, Reframe $100/yr — everyone charges above our $5. AI-companion market norms (2026): $9.99–14.99/mo gating voice/memory/customization; expect <5% free→paid conversion, plan around it; voice adds ~$1–3/mo per user in cost.

Lifetime option (~$199–299) for upfront cash

A one-time lifetime tier brings in runway cash now. Pair it with fair-use caps so ongoing token cost stays bounded.

Evidence: Replika sells a $299 lifetime tier.

Fair-use caps on the free tier

Cap messages on free, unlimited on paid — protects margin and creates a natural reason to upgrade.

Evidence: I Am Sober caps free habit-tracking; Sunflower gates unlimited messaging behind its subscription.

Lever 2 · Ascension — AI → human & clinical care

Biggest + most defensible (and most on-mission)

Warm handoff to human recovery coaching

When the AI detects the moment someone needs more than a chatbot, it can offer a vetted recovery coach. We take a referral cut or run the marketplace. Never a sales push — a well-timed offer of real help.

Evidence: coaches charge $200–400/hr; peer-coaching plans from $375/mo. Reframe's "Thrive Coaching" is this exact model.

Referral to teletherapy / medication treatment

Partner with licensed telehealth providers for the highest-value handoffs. Often the user pays little because insurance covers it — they get real care, we structure the partnership so it pays (see the legal note below for how).

Evidence: Monument $25/visit (insured), Ophelia / Bicycle Health ~$245/mo, insurance-reimbursed.

⚠️ Legal guardrail on clinical referrals: EKRA

Per-patient referral fees to clinical addiction treatment (treatment facilities, recovery homes, labs) are a federal crime under EKRA (Eliminating Kickbacks in Recovery Act, 2018) — it applies to all payers, including cash and private insurance, with penalties up to $200K / 10 years per occurrence. Several states (Florida most aggressively) add patient-brokering laws on top. This is why Sunflower owns its clinic instead of brokering referrals. What's safe: non-clinical coaching referrals/marketplace, flat sponsorship/licensing fees vetted by counsel, or eventually owning the care layer — never a per-patient cut on clinical care.

Lever 3 · Recovery-native products

High margin, culturally native (Matt's journal idea holds up)

Milestone "your story" journal

A $30–40 keepsake at sobriety anniversaries — the wins, reflections and milestones the sponsor captured over 6/12 months. Anniversaries are a huge deal in recovery → emotionally resonant, high margin.

Physical sobriety chips / medallions

The gamified digital chips → option to order the real chip, or gift one. The single most culturally native upsell — chips are sacred in the program.

Gifting

Let loved ones buy a subscription or a chip for someone in recovery. The worried family member often has the money and urgency. Doubles as growth and ties to the 1-for-1 mission.

Evidence: the sober-companion market is sold to family offices at $750–2,500/day — families pay for recovery support.

Lever 4 · Institutions & mission funding

The highest-ceiling plays for later

Treatment-center / sober-living aftercare licensing

White-label AI aftercare. Rehabs have budgets and a brutal readmission problem that AI aftercare directly reduces. Per-seat B2B dwarfs $5 consumer.

Evidence: this is Loosid's enterprise / EAP direction.

Nonprofit arm to fund the "give" side

A 501(c)(3) arm can tap SAMHSA grants (the SABG block grant + the Recovery Community Services peer-support program), foundations, and corporate CSR to sponsor the 1-for-1 scholarships. Matt already floated non-profit — this turns the free side into a funding engine and a PR story, not a cost.

Lever 5 · New markets on the same engine

From the July 2 deep dive — same product, bigger doors

The family SKU — sell to the worried spouse/parent

Nobody has built a strong digital product for the family members of people struggling — the Al-Anon / Nar-Anon audience. They have money, urgency, and no 24/7 support of their own. An "AI companion for families" (CRAFT-informed: the evidence-based method proven 6× more effective than Al-Anon at getting a loved one into treatment) is a second product on the exact same engine — and a growth loop: the family member subscribes, the loved one gets invited.

Evidence: CRAFT engages the loved one into treatment in ~7 of 10 families; the family/friends support space is still meetings-first, digitally underserved.

Sober-curious mode — 10× the audience

49% of Americans say they plan to drink less; the moderation apps (Sunnyside: 600K+ users; Reframe) monetize "gray-area drinkers" who will never identify as addicts or walk into a 12-step room. A "drink less" mode or sister brand on the same engine multiplies the addressable market far beyond diagnosed addiction — and it's a feeder: some moderation users discover they need the full sponsor.

Evidence: Dry January at 30% participation and climbing; non-alcoholic beverages passed $1B in US sales; Sunnyside even upsells naltrexone telehealth on top.

Faith channel — Celebrate Recovery's 35,000 churches

Celebrate Recovery runs Christ-centered 12-step ministries in 35,000+ churches with 5M+ lifetime participants — plus prisons, rescue missions and universities. A CR-flavored or church-white-label sponsor (their 8 principles + Christ-centered steps), distributed through ministries that sponsor access for their congregation, is a distribution channel nobody in the AI space is touching. Adjacent to Matt's Conscious AI audience.

Courts & diversion programs — B2G per-participant

Drug courts and DUI diversion programs mandate recovery activity and pay vendors for monitoring/education (a mature market — CorrectiveSolutions et al.). AI Sponsor as court-approved between-session support, sold per-participant to programs. Must be positioned as support, not surveillance — participant-consented engagement summaries only, or we torch the trust the product runs on.

Opioid settlement funds — a $57.7B pool being granted right now

States and counties are actively distributing $57.7B in opioid settlement money earmarked for treatment, recovery and prevention — e.g. Colorado just awarded $11M in one round. County-level grants for "24/7 AI recovery support access for residents" is a real, current application — and the natural funding engine for the 1-for-1 give side (pairs with the nonprofit arm in Lever 4).

Be the tool for Medicaid-billable peer support — not the billable thing

48 states + DC reimburse peer recovery support services ($25–40/hr). We don't bill Medicaid — certified peer specialists and recovery organizations do. AI Sponsor licensed per-seat to those organizations lets one peer specialist support a bigger caseload (AI covers 3am; the human covers the moments that matter), documented for their billing.

Cautionary tale baked in: Pear Therapeutics got FDA approval for reSET (digital SUD therapeutic), couldn't get payers to reimburse $1,300/course, and went bankrupt. Lesson: never build the business on insurers paying for the app itself — sell to the humans who already get paid.

Commerce & marketplace — the Loosid playbook (done our way)

Loosid layered a sober shop (~$70 average order), a brand-discount marketplace (members save ~$1,200/yr), and premium at $12.99 on top of community — and grew from $650K (2024) toward a projected $4.15M (2025). Their ad layer we skip (see the don't below), but a curated shop + partner discounts is value-add, not extraction — and pairs naturally with chips, journals and gifting in Lever 3.

The one clear "don't"

⚠️ No ads, no data-selling

Character.AI rolled out intrusive full-screen ads in 2026 and damaged its own product. In recovery, trust is the entire asset. We monetize through value — care, personalization, keepsakes — never through attention or data.

What to pursue first

Honest ranking
  1. Fix the launch structure now — raise the Anthropic spend cap + alerts, verify caching, and set the tier pyramid ($5–9 Standard on a cheaper model / $15–19 Premium on the best model) before Stripe goes live. Free to do today; expensive to do after we have subscribers.
  2. AI-as-front-door to coaching / clinical care — biggest + most defensible; what the funded players are building. Coaching first (no EKRA exposure), clinical partnerships only with counsel-vetted flat-fee structures.
  3. Milestone journal + chips + gifting + curated shop — Matt's instinct, high margin, tasteful; Loosid proves community + commerce compounds.
  4. Family SKU + sober-curious mode — the two biggest audience multipliers on the same engine; family side also feeds growth.
  5. Institutions & mission money — treatment-center aftercare, peer-support per-seat licensing, Celebrate Recovery channel, courts, opioid settlement grants + nonprofit arm. Highest ceiling; needs sales motion; for later.
